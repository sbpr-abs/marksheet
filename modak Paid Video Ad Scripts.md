# Modak — Paid Video Ad Scripts & Production Guide

## Ad Framework (Every Video)
1. **Hook (0-3s):** Stop the scroll. Pattern interrupt. Emotional or visual shock.
2. **Problem (3-8s):** Identify the pain. Make the parent feel seen.
3. **Solution (8-18s):** Introduce Modak as the escape route. Show, don't tell.
4. **Proof (18-25s):** Real reaction. Real child. Real moment.
5. **CTA (25-30s):** Single action. Urgency or ease.

## Ad 1: "Bedtime War" (Vertical, 30s)
**Primary use:** Instagram Reels, Facebook Reels, TikTok
**Objective:** App installs — bedtime audience

### Script
```
[0:00-0:03] HOOK
(Shot: Parent staring exhausted into camera. Child screaming off-screen.)
PARENT (direct, almost breaking fourth wall): "Bedtime's been war for two years."
TEXT OVERLAY: "Every. Single. Night."

[0:03-0:07] PROBLEM
(Quick cuts: child negotiating, hiding, crying on floor. Real chaos.)
PARENT (voiceover): "Bath, book, beg, plead. Finally bribe them with a loud cartoon just to get 20 minutes of peace."

[0:07-0:13] SOLUTION
(Transition: soft lighting. Same living room. Parent hands child a tablet with Modak open.)
PARENT (quieter, hopeful): "Then I found this."
(Child immediately stills. Narration from app audible: gentle voice telling Ganesha story.)

[0:13-0:22] PROOF
(Close-up: child's face calm, almost asleep. Parent in background, drinking actual hot coffee.)
PARENT (to camera, amazed): "Same child. Same room. Different human being."
CHILD (half-asleep, mumbling): "...Ganesha listened first..."

[0:22-0:27] CTA BUILD
(Shot: phone screen, Modak app, "Start Free Story" button.)
PARENT: "First three stories are free. No credit card."

[0:27-0:30] CTA
TEXT OVERLAY (bold): "Try tonight. Sleep better."
(Modak logo. App Store + Play Store. QR code flashes.)
```

### Production Notes
- **Cast:** Real parent + real child (or parent-child duo influencers). Authenticity > polish.
- **Lighting:** First half fluorescent/harsh. Second half warm lamp/soft golden glow.
- **Audio:** First half chaotic overlapping noise. Second half silence except for Modak narration.
- **Text overlays:** All caps, bold sans-serif, yellow on black for hook. Clean white for CTA.
- **Shooting:** Handheld/shaky for chaos. Locked tripod for calm half.
- **Music:** None. Let the audio contrast do the work.

## Ad 2: "I Forgot the Ramayana" (Vertical, 30s)
**Primary use:** Instagram Reels, Facebook in-feed
**Objective:** Subscriptions — cultural guilt audience

### Script
```
[0:00-0:04] HOOK
(Shot: Parent alone in kitchen, confessional style. Raw.)
PARENT: "I want my son to know our stories."
(beat, looks down)
"But if you asked me to tell the Ramayana right now... I'd fail."

[0:04-0:09] PROBLEM
(Cut to dining table. Parent awkwardly trying to tell story to blank-faced child.)
PARENT: "I tried. He looked confused. I felt like I was failing at culture."

[0:09-0:16] SOLUTION
(Transition: Parent and child together on couch, both watching Modak. Child leans in.)
PARENT: "So we discovered it together. Both of us, first time."
CHILD: "Papa, why did Ravana take Sita if he knew it was wrong?"

[0:16-0:21] PROOF
(Parent's genuine jaw-drop. Camera holds the beat.)
PARENT: "My five-year-old understood dharma before I explained the word."

[0:21-0:25] REASSURANCE
(Back to parent confessional, more confident now.)
PARENT: "You don't need to know it all. You just need to be willing to learn with them."

[0:25-0:30] CTA
TEXT: "Stories that teach both of you."
(Phone screen: Modak open, parent and child profile icons visible.)
PARENT: "Start free tonight. Link in bio."
```

### Production Notes
- **Cast:** Indian millennial parent (25-35). Child 4-6 years old. Warm, not model-beautiful.
- **Setting:** Real home, not a set. Visible normal clutter. Authenticity is the message.
- **Lighting:** Natural window light in kitchen. Warm tungsten in living room.
- **Audio:** Natural room tone. No music bed. Crisp voiceover.
- **Key moment:** The child's question must feel unscripted. Leave camera rolling, capture the real one.
- **Color grading:** Slightly desaturated in the "problem" section. Warm, slightly golden in the "solution" section.

## Ad 3: "The Screen Time I Don't Hide" (Vertical, 30s)
**Primary use:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
**Objective:** App installs — guilt-driven parents

### Script
```
[0:00-0:03] HOOK
(Shot: Parent looking guilty, hiding tablet behind back as if caught.)
PARENT: "I used to hand my kid YouTube Kids and then hide from the mom group chat."
(beat)
"Because I knew."

[0:03-0:08] PROBLEM
(Split screen or quick cuts: hyperactive child post-cartoon vs. guilty parent scrolling on phone.)
PARENT: "Loud colors. Flashing ads. Zero takeaway. And I felt every minute."

[0:08-0:16] SOLUTION
(Soft transition. Same parent, same couch, now calm. Child beside them, absorbed in Modak. Slow pan.)
PARENT (voiceover): "Now I give him stories about gods who teach patience. Courage. Keeping your word."

[0:16-0:22] PROOF
(Close-up: child explains something to parent, parent looks impressed.)
CHILD: "Dharma means doing the right thing even when nobody sees."
PARENT (to camera, smiling): "He's five. He said that. Not me."

[0:22-0:27] BENEFIT
(Parent now doing peaceful activity — folding clothes, drinking tea — while child watches.)
PARENT: "And I finally get twenty minutes without guilt."

[0:27-0:30] CTA
TEXT: "Approved screen time. Free to start."
(Modak logo. App Store + Play Store. Swipe up.)
```

### Production Notes
- **Visually:** The "hiding" gesture in the hook must feel comedic, not dark. Light shame, not heavy.
- **Split screen:** Use real footage, not stock. The contrast is the entire ad.
- **Child delivery:** The "dharma" line is the money shot. If the child can't say it naturally, use a 6-year-old who can, or rewrite to a simpler moral the child CAN say.
- **Pacing:** Deliberate. Let silences land. This is not a fast-cut ad.
- **End card:** Clean. Minimal. Trust-building, not hype-building.

## Ad 4: "Voice I Miss" (Vertical, 30s)
**Primary use:** Instagram Reels, Facebook Reels
**Objective:** Subscriptions — nostalgia/emotion audience

### Script
```
[0:00-0:05] HOOK
(Shot: Parent alone at night, dim light. Emotional, quiet.)
PARENT: "My dadi used to tell me stories."
(beat, looks away)
"Now my daughter only sees her on video call. Twice a month."

[0:05-0:10] PROBLEM
(Cut: child in bed, looking at phone with grandparent on screen. Grandparent waving goodbye. Child looks sad.)
PARENT: "There's a gap. And I don't know how to fill it."

[0:10-0:19] SOLUTION
(Soft transition. Parent opens Modak. Narration starts: warm, slightly crackly, grandmother-like voice.)
PARENT (voiceover): "This narrator... she reminds me of someone."
(Shot: child in bed, listening, slowly smiling. Not hyper — genuinely comforted.)
PARENT (whisper, to camera): "It's not her lap. But it's still sacred."

[0:19-0:24] PROOF
(Child opens eyes, looks at parent.)
CHILD: "Mama, can we hear the one about Ganesha again tomorrow?"
PARENT (smiles, emotional): "Yes, baby. Every night if you want."

[0:24-0:30] CTA
TEXT (gentle): "For the nights you miss her voice."
(Modak logo. Warm glow. App icons.)
VOICEOVER: "First story free. Download Modak."
```

### Production Notes
- **Tone:** This is the emotional ad. Do not rush it. Every pause is intentional.
- **Narration casting:** The Modak narrator MUST feel like a 60+ Indian woman with a warm, unhurried voice. This ad fails if the voice sounds young or commercial.
- **Child's face:** Not smiling broadly — soft, safe, drifting. Sleepy-comforted, not entertained.
- **Music:** Single instrument, maybe a soft bansuri or tanpura drone. Almost inaudible.
- **Color grade:** Muted tones, warm highlights, slight vignette.
- **Risk:** If this feels too sad, it won't convert. The parent's final smile must signal hope, not despair.

## Ad 5: "The Quiz Ad" (Vertical, 30s)
**Primary use:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
**Objective:** Engagement shares — viral/comedy format

### Script
```
[0:00-0:03] HOOK
(Shot: parent and child side by side. Child looks confident. Parent looks nervous.)
PARENT: "My 6-year-old is about to school me on Hindu mythology."
CHILD (grinning): "I'm ready."

[0:03-0:07] ROUND ONE
PARENT: "Who is Hanuman?"
CHILD (too fast, too smart): "Monkey god. Incredibly strong. Jumped across the ocean to find Sita because he promised."

[0:07-0:10] PARENT REACTS
(Parent glances at camera, shocked. Comedic hold.)
PARENT: "Okay. That was... that was detailed."

[0:10-0:16] ROUND TWO
PARENT: "Fine. Why did he do it?"
CHILD (serious now): "Because a promise is dharma. Even when it's hard. Especially when it's hard."

[0:16-0:21] MONEY SHOT
(Parent's face: genuine speechlessness. Beat. Then looks at camera.)
PARENT: "I didn't teach her that."
(beat)
"Modak did."

[0:21-0:27] SETUP FOR CTA
(Parent puts arm around child, both look at camera.)
PARENT: "Honestly? I learn as much as she does."
CHILD: "She definitely does."

[0:27-0:30] CTA
TEXT: "Stories that stick. Free to start."
(Modak logo. App icons.)
```

### Production Notes
- **This ad lives or dies on the child's delivery.** Cast first, script second. Find the kid who can nail "especially when it's hard" with gravitas.
- **Parent reaction:** Must be genuine. No acting school graduate — a real parent whose real kid said this. Or cast a parent who can genuinely look shocked.
- **Pacing:** Snappy. Fast cuts. TikTok rhythm. This is the opposite of the "Voice I Miss" ad.
- **Text overlays:** Question text on screen during each round. Answer text appears with a ding sound.
- **Music:** Light, modern, almost royalty-free elevator beat. Nothing serious.
- **Comments strategy:** Pin a comment: "Drop a 🔥 if your kid could do this."
- **Hook variant for A/B test:** Start with the child's answer first, parent reaction second. See which stops the scroll faster.

## Ad 6: "The Bumper" (Horizontal/Vertical, 6 seconds)
**Primary use:** YouTube pre-roll (skippable), Instagram Stories
**Objective:** Awareness / brand recall

### Script
```
[0:00-0:02]
(Black screen. Child's voice, clear and surprising.)
CHILD (VO): "Dharma means keeping your promise even when nobody's watching."

[0:02-0:04]
(Cut to parent, startled, looking at child off-camera.)
PARENT: "Where did you—"

[0:04-0:06]
(TEXT over soft gold background:)
"Modak. Stories that teach."
"First one free."
(Modak logo.)
```

### Production Notes
- **Audio-first design:** This must work even if the user is looking at another tab. The line must be arresting.
- **Visual:** Minimal. The shock on the parent's face is all the storytelling needed.
- **Frequency capping:** Do not serve this more than 3x per user per week. Repetition kills the magic of the line.

## Ad 7: "The Proof Ad" (Horizontal/Vertical, 60 seconds)
**Primary use:** YouTube in-feed, Facebook in-feed, landing page
**Objective:** Full funnel — consideration to install

### Script
```
[0:00-0:05] HOOK
(Open on parent in kitchen. Direct, honest, slightly tired.)
PARENT: "I used to think screen time was always bad."
(beat, puts down mug)
"Then I had a 5-year-old. And a deadline. And 20 minutes where I just needed to exist."

[0:05-0:12] PROBLEM DEEPENED
(Cut: montage of real parenting moments — cooking with one hand, answering email at counter, looking exhausted at 7 PM.)
PARENT (voiceover): "Every mom group says 'limit screens.' But nobody says what to do when you're on fire and your kid won't quit."

[0:12-0:20] SOLUTION INTRODUCED
(Soft transition. Parent opens Modak on tablet. Hands to child.)
PARENT (voiceover): "So I tried this instead of the usual loud stuff."
(Child immediately stills. Narration starts. Soft, warm, mythological story about Rama.)

[0:20-0:35] PROOF BUILDING
(Sequence of three brief clips:)
- CHILD at dinner table, to parent: "Rama didn't break his promise even when it was hard. So I shouldn't either."
- CHILD to friend: "Sharing is dharma. You should share your blocks."
- PARENT (to camera, quietly moved): "Three days after one story. He used the word dharma. I didn't teach him that."

[0:35-0:45] SOLUTION REINFORCED
(Show app interface: clean, beautiful, Sanskrit names pronounced correctly, art like moving paintings.)
PARENT: "It's not preachy. It's not boring. It's just... real stories, beautifully told."

[0:45-0:53] OBJECTION HANDLER
(Parent back in kitchen, now more confident.)
PARENT: "First three stories are free. No card needed. If your kid doesn't quote something back in a week, delete it."
(smiles)
"But mine did."

[0:53-0:60] CTA
(TEXT: "Download Modak. Free stories inside.")
(App Store. Play Store. QR code.)
(PARENT, off-screen, laughing: "He just asked for the next story.")
```

### Production Notes
- **This is the flagship ad.** It needs the most production value, but still feel real.
- **The three proof clips:** Can be shot with the same family or three different real Modak families. Diversity matters here. Show different ages, genders, and home types.
- **App demo:** 10-second screen recording of actual Modak UI. Beautiful, smooth, clean.
- **The "quote back" test:** This is the brand's core promise. The ad must make viewers believe it happens.
- **End with laughter:** The parent's off-screen laugh humanizes the brand. Modak is serious about values, but not solemn.
- **Color grade:** Warm gold throughout. Consistent with brand. No cold blues.

## Creative Variations & A/B Testing Matrix

| Ad Version | Element to Test | Variation A | Variation B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bedtime War | **Hook** | Audio chaos first | Parent's exhausted face first |
| Forgot Ramayana | **Emotion** | Vulnerable/shame | Hopeful/pride |
| Screen Time | **Proof** | Child quote | Parent free-time montage |
| Voice I Miss | **Voice actor** | Elderly woman (60+) | Middle-aged mother (35+) |
| Quiz Ad | **Parent gender** | Mom | Dad |
| Bumper | **Child quote** | Dharma line | Karma line |

### Testing Cadence
- Run each ad minimum 7 days before judging performance.
- Primary metric: Cost Per Install (CPI) by creative.
- Secondary: 7-day retention rate by creative (not just installs).
- Kill any ad with CPI > ₹120 after 5,000 impressions. Iterate, don't just delete.

## Video Production Checklist

### Pre-Production
- [ ] Cast real parent-child pair (or micro-influencer duo with existing chemistry)
- [ ] Scout real homes, not studios. Clutter is texture. Perfection is suspicion.
- [ ] Record Modak narration to play on set so child's reactions are real
- [ ] Prepare 3x more questions than script requires (for ad-lib gold)
- [ ] Brief talent: "We want the messy take, not the polished one."

### Production
- [ ] Shoot vertical 9:16 primary, horizontal 16:9 cutdown secondary
- [ ] Capture room tone / silence from each location (for contrast edits)
- [ ] Record "clean" Modak audio separately (high quality, no room echo)
- [ ] Leave camera rolling 10 seconds after every take (best reactions are unguarded)
- [ ] Capture B-roll: hands, tablet screen, child's eyes, parent breathing

### Post-Production
- [ ] First cut: longer than needed. Cut ruthlessly. If a line doesn't land, kill it.
- [ ] Audio mix: narration must be clear over music. Always.
- [ ] Color grade: warm gold family. Never cold corporate blue.
- [ ] Text overlays: readable on mobile without sound. Test on a phone, not a monitor.
- [ ] Export: 1080x1920 (vertical), 1920x1080 (horizontal), 4K master for future cropping

## Legal & Platform Notes

- **Disclosure:** Include "#ad" or "Sponsored" overlay in first 3 seconds for all influencer-cut versions.
- **Child talent:** Parent/guardian signed release required. Minors cannot appear in ads without consent in most markets.
- **App Store compliance:** If showing iPhone, must also show Android. Alternate frames or split end card.
- **Claim substantiation:** The "dharma" quote is a real user submission. Keep documentation. Do not script fictional child quotes for legal ads without "actor portrayal" disclaimer.
- **Music licensing:** All music must be licensed for paid social use. Prefer original or royalty-free to avoid regional claim issues.
